Case Study 01 · Sales Operations

CRM & Pipeline Management

Client: Elevate SaaS Solutions — B2B project management tool for remote teams ($49–$499/month)

HubSpot Sales Hub Zapier Google Workspace

The situation

Elevate SaaS Solutions had a real pipeline problem. Two SDRs and one account executive were tracking 8+ active deals in a shared Google Sheet with no visibility into who owned which lead, what stage each deal was at, or which prospects had gone cold. Deals were slipping not because of poor selling — but because the system behind the selling was broken.

What I built

I set up a full HubSpot CRM pipeline from scratch: structured contact records with tags and activity logs, and custom deal stages mapped to Elevate's actual sales motion — New Lead → First Outreach Sent → Discovery Call Booked → Demo Completed → Proposal Sent → Negotiation → Closed — with probability weighting at each stage so the team could forecast revenue accurately.

I built a 5-step cold outreach sequence targeting Operations Managers and COOs at companies with 10–200 employees. All five emails automated across 15 days, with personalized variables, timed delays, and unenrollment triggers for prospects who replied. I configured a round-robin meeting booking page with calendar sync, intake questions, and automated 1-hour pre-call reminder emails.

Finally, I built a weekly pipeline report and drafted a recurring update email for the sales manager — covering open deals by stage, revenue forecast by owner, and flagged contacts with no activity in 5+ days. Turned raw HubSpot data into a Monday morning decision tool.

Key outcomes

  • Full pipeline visibility9 custom deal stages with probability tracking and deal owner assignment — replaced a spreadsheet where deals were falling through the gaps weekly.
  • Automated outreach sequence5-step, 15-day cold email sequence with 100% automated steps, personalized variables, and A/B test capability built in from Day 1.
  • Meeting scheduling systemRound-robin discovery call booking with intake pre-qualification questions, Google Meet links, and automated 1-hour reminder emails — zero manual coordination.
  • Weekly pipeline reportingRecurring report covering deal health, revenue forecast by owner, and flagged stale contacts — gave the sales manager a clear weekly decision tool.
  • Complete sales infrastructureWent from a broken spreadsheet to a fully operational HubSpot setup — contacts, deals, sequences, scheduling, and reporting all connected.

Case Study 02 · Lead Generation & Strategy

Lead Generation Strategy & Analysis

Client: JOOCYEE (@joocyeeindonesia) — Indonesian C-beauty brand on Instagram & Shopee

Apollo Excel Research Tools AI Tools

The situation

The client was a local Indonesian C-beauty brand with strong product quality but no structured approach to finding and reaching potential stockists, collaborators, or wholesale buyers. Outreach had been inconsistent and untargeted — built on guesswork rather than data. Growth had plateaued not because the product was weak, but because the lead generation strategy behind it was missing entirely.

What I did

I began by mapping the competitive landscape — analyzing JOOCYEE's positioning against direct and indirect competitors across pricing, audience targeting, social proof, and channel strategy. I identified where the brand's messaging was misaligned with how their target audience actually made purchasing decisions on TikTok Shop and Shopee.

Using Apollo and manual research, I built a segmented prospect database of 3,000+ contacts filtered by industry, company size, platform presence, and buyer behavior signals. For each segment I developed a personalized outreach angle based on the contact's business context — referencing their LinkedIn activity, company growth signals, and specific pain points relevant to their role.

I delivered a structured strategic recommendation report prioritizing lead generation actions by implementation difficulty and expected impact — giving the client a clear roadmap they could act on immediately, not just a data dump.

Key outcomes

  • Competitive analysisFull competitor audit covering product positioning, pricing, social proof, and channel distribution — surfaced 4 specific positioning gaps the client could act on immediately.
  • Audience behavior mappingMapped customer motivations, decision triggers, and engagement barriers — produced an insight-led targeting framework aligned with actual buyer behavior.
  • Apollo lead list — 3,000+ contactsSegmented prospect database with verified contact data, industry and location filters, Shopify store detection, and a custom outreach angle column for each contact type.
  • Channel & conversion auditAudited website, Instagram, and Shopee presence to identify conversion bottlenecks and where leads were dropping off.
  • Prioritized strategy roadmapActionable recommendations ranked by implementation priority — a clear picture of who to target, how to reach them, and what to fix first.

How I work

My SDR methodology

The process I follow for every prospecting campaign — from first research to first reply.

Step 01

Research & Segment

Start in Apollo — filtering by industry, location, company size, and buying intent signals. Every lead list is segmented before a single email is written. I research each prospect's LinkedIn and company context to develop a personalized outreach angle before outreach begins.

Step 02

Build the Sequence

Multi-touch outreach sequences in HubSpot — typically 5 steps across 15 days, mixing automated emails with manual call tasks and LinkedIn touches. Every email has a distinct purpose: question, value, proof, low-friction ask, close. Subject lines and personalization tokens set before enrollment.

Step 03

Execute & Track

Every outreach touch is logged — channel, step, date, status — in a structured tracker. I monitor reply rates, open rates, and bounce rates by sequence step to identify where prospects are dropping off and adjust. No lead falls through without a documented reason.

Step 04

Report & Iterate

Weekly pipeline reports pulled from HubSpot and delivered as a written summary — covering what's moving, what's stalled, and what needs attention. When sequences underperform, I test subject line variants and adjust timing before re-enrolling cold contacts.
